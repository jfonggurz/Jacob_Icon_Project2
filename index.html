A New York City Cycling Guide

A compilation of resources for cycling in New York City

By Jacob Fong-Gurzinsky

There are over 762,000 New Yorkers who cycle on a frequent basis, as found by the Community Health Survey from the New York City Department of Health and Mental Health (NYC DOH) and listed on the New York City Department of Transportation (NYC DOT) website. The NYC DOH also said on its website that cycling allows people to get needed exercise and lists specific ways the activity can help people’s health, one of which is preventing diabetes.

With the many cyclers in New York City and the recommendation to do this activity, New Yorkers need a comprehensive guide to help them. There is a lot of information and resources for cyclers, but this guide will provide you with one place to access useful and interesting information.

Safety and Legal Considerations

This section provides government and other guides to help you cycle safely.

  • This document is New York City’s cycling guide from the NYC DOT to cycling and includes helpful graphics explaining cycling rules. It also includes links to other cycling organizations and clubs.
  • This webpage from the NYC DOH provides more cycling safety tips and helpful resources, such as links to more safety information, including both government data and maps.
  • This webpage from NYC DOT also provides graphics with safety information, helpful links for wearing a helmet properly, and a sign-up for their cycling newsletter.

Cycling Routes

This section provides cycling route guides for New York City.

  • The Ride the City website provides cycling guides for cities around the world, including New York City. It is recommended by Bicycle Habitat (bicycle store) on its website.
  • This article by Francesca Carington for Condé Nast Traveler lists routes recommended by the author to see different parts of New York City.
  • This webpage on the Ride with GPS website provides New York City cycling routes with elevation information and pictures for some of the routes.
  • This guide from the NYC DOT lists one family-friendly cycling route for each borough in New York City.
